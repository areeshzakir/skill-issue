Executing Plans takes a structured implementation plan (typically generated by Writing Plans) and works through it step by step, implementing each task, running tests, and pausing at designated review checkpoints for human approval before proceeding. It maintains awareness of the overall plan state, tracks completed and remaining steps, and adapts when implementation reveals that a plan step needs modification.

Use Executing Plans when you have a detailed implementation plan ready and want methodical, reviewable execution rather than implementing everything in one shot. It is ideal for complex multi-step features where you want to verify each phase before moving to the next, reducing the risk of having to unwind large amounts of work if an early assumption was wrong.

Example: Feed Executing Plans the 15-step billing implementation plan. It begins with step 1 (database migration), implements it, runs the migration test, and pauses for your review. Upon approval, it proceeds to step 2 (BillingService), implements the service with tests, and pauses again. At step 7 (a designated checkpoint), it provides a summary of all completed work and asks whether to continue or adjust the remaining plan based on what was learned during implementation.
