Brainstorming facilitates a structured collaborative dialogue that transforms rough ideas into fully formed designs and plans. It uses techniques like divergent/convergent thinking, "yes, and" building, constraint identification, and feasibility assessment to take an initial concept through ideation, refinement, and specification. The output is a clear design document ready for implementation planning.

Use Brainstorming at the start of a new project, feature, or initiative when you have a general direction but need to explore the solution space, identify constraints, and converge on a specific approach. It is especially valuable when working solo and you need a thinking partner to challenge assumptions, surface edge cases, and push ideas further than you would alone.

Example: Start Brainstorming with "I want to add a notification system to our app." The skill explores questions like: what events trigger notifications, in-app vs push vs email, real-time vs batched, user preference controls, and quiet hours. Through iterative dialogue, it converges on a specific design: a tiered notification system with urgency levels, user-configurable channels per event type, and a digest mode for non-critical updates. The session concludes with a structured design document.
