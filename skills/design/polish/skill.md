Polish performs a meticulous final quality pass on your interface, catching the subtle details that distinguish professional work from rough implementation. It examines pixel-level alignment, consistent spacing rhythms, typography line heights, icon sizing uniformity, shadow consistency, border treatments, hover state completeness, and visual balance across the entire page.

Use Polish as the last step before shipping a feature or presenting a design for stakeholder review. It catches the kind of details that are individually minor but collectively determine whether an interface feels crafted or cobbled together. Think of it as the design equivalent of a code linter.

Example: Run Polish on your completed settings page to discover that the section headers use inconsistent bottom margins (16px, 20px, and 24px), two icons are 2px larger than the rest, the cancel button's hover state is missing, and the form labels are not baseline-aligned with their input fields. Each issue is flagged with its exact location and the precise fix needed.
