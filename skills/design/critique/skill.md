Critique provides a structured UX evaluation of your interface, assessing it through the lens of established design heuristics, user psychology, and interaction design principles. It examines information architecture, visual hierarchy, cognitive load, task flow efficiency, affordances, and consistency. The output is a balanced review highlighting both strengths and areas for improvement with specific, actionable recommendations.

Use Critique when you want an objective second opinion on a design before presenting it to stakeholders, during design reviews, or when you are too close to a project to see its weaknesses. It is especially useful for solo designers who lack a team to provide regular feedback.

Example: Submit your e-commerce product page for Critique to receive feedback such as: the add-to-cart button competes visually with the wishlist button (recommendation: increase size differential), the review section is below the fold which may reduce trust signals, and the size selector lacks a clear default state which creates decision paralysis.
